What are some Atlanta Braves Traditions?




The Chop

During key moments at Truist Park, fans raise their arms and rhythmically perform the iconic Chop chant that echoes through the stadium. At night games, it becomes even more powerful as fans wave their phone flashlights in unison, lighting up the park in a stunning display of unity and Braves pride. Although this tradition, and the accompanying music, has been officially banned, Braves fans continue to perform the chant themselves.

7th Inning Stretch

During the 7th inning stretch at Braves home games, fans come alive with clapping, dancing, and singing along to John Denver’s "Thank God I'm A Country Boy." The energy continues with a lovely rendition of "God Bless America," performed by renowned tenor Timothy Miller, creating a powerful moment of unity and hometown pride.

Beat the Freeze

One lucky fan races "The Freeze", a sprinting superhero in goggles, spandex, and a blue bodysuit. He lets fans get a head start — then smokes them down the warning track in a hilarious finish.

Friday Night Fireworks

Along with every home run scored, the Friday Night Fireworks in a Truist Park staple. Fireworks light up the sky after Friday home games all season long.

Meet the Mascot

Say hello to Blooper, the Braves’ lovable mischief-maker! Whether he’s ziplining into the stadium, photobombing fans, or causing chaos on the dugout, Blooper brings wild energy to every home game.

More than just a mascot, he’s a crowd-favorite, TikTok star, and the spirit animal of Braves Country.
