What are some Atlanta Braves Traditions?

The Chop

During key moments at Truist Park, fans raise their arms and rhythmically perform the iconic Chop chant that echoes through the stadium. At night games, it becomes even more powerful as fans wave their phone flashlights in unison, lighting up the park in a stunning display of unity and Braves pride. Although this tradition, and the accompanying music, has been officially banned, Braves fans continue to perform the chant themselves.

7th Inning Stretch

During the 7th inning stretch at Braves home games, fans come alive with clapping, dancing, and singing along to John Denver’s "Thank God I'm A Country Boy." The energy continues with a lovely rendition of "God Bless America," performed by renowned tenor Timothy Miller, creating a powerful moment of unity and hometown pride.

Beat the Freeze

One lucky fan races "The Freeze", a sprinting superhero in goggles, spandex, and a blue bodysuit. He lets fans get a head start — then smokes them down the warning track in a hilarious finish.

Friday Night Fireworks

Along with every home run scored, the Friday Night Fireworks in a Truist Park staple. Fireworks light up the sky after Friday home games all season long.

Meet the Mascot

Blooper

Say hello to Blooper, the Braves’ official mascot and the lovable mischief-maker! He was created in 2018 at the Braves fan fest and is known for his antics and ability to entertain fans both at Truist Park and in the community. Blooper's popularity has grown significantly, especially during the 2020 season when he created fun routines for fans at home. Whether he’s ziplining into the stadium, photobombing fans, running the braves flag over the field, dancing with players, or causing chaos on the dugout, Blooper brings wild energy to every home game.

More than just a mascot, he’s a crowd-favorite, TikTok star, and the spirit animal of Braves Country.

Braves in Pop Culture

🎬 Movies & TV

  • The Slugger’s Wife (1985) – A Neil Simon comedy-drama featuring a fictional Braves slugger and scenes at Atlanta–Fulton County Stadium.
  • Major League II (1994) – Braves’ old stadium stood in as the Cleveland Indians’ home field for several scenes.
  • The Office (S5 Ep23) – Ryan wears a retro Braves cap at the bowling alley.
  • The Walking Dead – Braves gear pops up in scenes, thanks to Georgia filming locations.
  • Cobra Kai – Braves memorabilia is sprinkled into scenes as a nod to ATL roots.

🎵 Music

  • Morgan Wallen – “’98 Braves” (2023) – A heartbreak anthem comparing a breakup to the Braves' 1998 postseason loss.
  • Ludacris – “Welcome to Atlanta” – A hometown anthem repping the Braves alongside other ATL icons.
  • Migos, Outkast, Future – All these Atlanta artists rock Braves gear, embedding the team into Atlanta's music culture.
  • Jason Aldean – “1994” – Drops references to Chipper Jones and the Braves.
  • Take Me Out to the Ballgame (Braves Remix) – ATL artists remixing the classic during playoff hype seasons.

📱 Memes & Internet Culture

  • Blooper the Mascot – A viral legend. Whether he’s failing flips, dance-battling fans, or doing back alley shenanigans – Blooper is Braves meme culture.
  • “The Braves Just Won the World Series” (2021) – Sparked a viral meme format across Twitter and TikTok, used for humorously celebrating long-awaited wins.
  • Braves Twitter – Chaotic good. Known for trolling rivals (looking at you, Mets), rain delay antics, and playoff rally energy. #ForTheA
  • “For the A” – Originally a slogan, now a lifestyle. Spawned merch, GIFs, memes, and even tattoos.
  • Helmet Flip TikToks – Fans recreate Travis d’Arnaud’s helmet fall mid-swing in viral trends.

🌟 Fashion & Celebrity Fans

  • Kanye West, Drake, Rihanna, Jay-Z – All spotted wearing the iconic “A” cap, often a vintage or throwback navy-and-red style.
  • Donald Glover (Childish Gambino) – Reps the Braves often, tying his ATL roots into interviews and performances.
  • Zendaya & Tom Holland – Went viral attending a Braves game during the Spider-Man: No Way Home tour.

Furry Friends of Braves Country

The Atlanta Braves don’t just show love for their players – they extend that affection to their furry companions too. From clubhouse pups to foundation-supported rescue efforts, the Braves celebrate the special bond between athletes and their pets. Fans are part of the pack as well, with pet-themed merch, events like Bark in the Park, and heartwarming features of their furry friends. For the Braves, pets are family—and family is everything.

Honorable Mention: Snitbear

A plush bear with a coaching legacy, Snitbear became a clubhouse icon during the 2021 World Series run. The players brought him everywhere — even Ozuna gave him a chain. He’s a fan favorite and the unofficial team hype man (or bear).

Ozzie Albies Foundation

Founded by Braves star Ozzie Albies, this foundation supports animal rescues, no-kill shelters, and adoption services. Ozzie’s love for animals extends far beyond the field—his goal is to make every furry friend feel safe and loved.
